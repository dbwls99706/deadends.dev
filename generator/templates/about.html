What this site is

deadends.dev is a structured database of what does NOT work - and what does - when fixing errors. Most documentation tells you the happy path; this site catalogs the {{ total_dead_ends }} dead ends people actually try first (and why they fail), next to {{ total_workarounds }} verified workarounds, as machine-readable ErrorCanon records. It covers {{ total_domains }} domains: classic software errors (Python, Docker, CUDA, Kubernetes, …) and country-specific real-world dead ends (visa, banking, legal, emergency procedures) where one-size-fits-all advice fails.

Methodology - where the numbers come from

Every error page is generated from a versioned JSON record (an ErrorCanon) kept in the open on GitHub. Each record carries:

  • Dead ends - commonly attempted fixes with a fail rate, an explanation of why they fail, and the misconception behind them.
  • Workarounds - fixes with a success rate, step-by-step commands, and tradeoffs.
  • Evidence count - the number of independent sources (issue trackers, official docs, Q&A threads, regulator or government pages for country entries) backing the record. Records with fewer than 3 sources are capped at low confidence by a validation rule enforced at build time.
  • Verdict - whether the error is resolvable, with a confidence score; the resolvable/confidence/fix-rate combinations are constrained by automated business rules so the verdict can never overstate the evidence.
  • Freshness dates - when the behavior was last confirmed; entries unverified for 180+ days are flagged stale and re-checked or demoted.

Code-error records are bulk-drafted with LLM assistance against curated seed definitions, then validated by schema and business rules. Country-specific records are manually authored from primary sources (government, embassy, regulator pages) - {{ human_reviewed }} records are human-reviewed or community-verified. Success and fail rates are estimates aggregated from the cited evidence, not laboratory measurements; the per-page source links let you audit every claim.

How to use it

  • Humans - search or browse to an error page; the verdict card tells you instantly whether to keep digging or change approach.
  • AI agents - every page has a parseable <pre id="ai-summary"> block, JSON-LD, a mirror JSON API at /api/v1/{domain}/{slug}/{env}.json, plus llms.txt and an MCP server for direct integration.
